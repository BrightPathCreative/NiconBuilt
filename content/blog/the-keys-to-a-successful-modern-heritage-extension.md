### Balancing preservation and practicality

While heritage homes are brimming with beauty and vintage charm, they sometimes lack modern convenience. This is an important thing to rectify, especially in an era where working from home is becoming the norm.

Common contributing factors to extension requests include:

- Extra interior space (bedrooms, bathrooms, theatre, playroom, office)
- Upgrade of facilities
- Improved natural lighting
- Fluid connection to outdoor areas
- Design modernisation within heritage constraints
- Maximised land use

Nicon Built are licensed building practitioners who view each heritage home extension as a unique, inspiring project requiring specialist care.

Achieving modern living with a period façade means creating a tailored strategy — one that addresses your daily needs, while preserving the aesthetic aspects of the heritage build. Changes must be made in accordance with the rules to avoid penalties and to pay respect to your home's history.

Having a defined vision from the outset is recommended. This way you won't introduce late-stage changes that risk breaching legislation. Heritage homes are protected, and gaining the required permits can lengthen the project schedule.

### The regulatory factor

Both state and local authorities have legislation dealing with the protection of heritage structures. The broad umbrella of heritage includes different categories, and regulations vary depending on location and scope of alterations.

As a starting point, visit [Heritage Victoria](https://www.planning.vic.gov.au/policy-and-strategy/local-heritage-protection) for an idea of the necessary permits and approvals. Some homeowners hesitate when confronted with council regulations pertaining to heritage-listed buildings — but you don't have to do this alone.

### The heritage difference

On average, a heritage extension requires a higher budget and longer timeframe compared to a standard extension. Part of this is due to specialist subcontractors and, in some cases, the sourcing of period-specific materials.

Permit approval may be slow, and the era of the original build could necessitate time-consuming construction techniques. Keep in mind, adding contemporary elements to a heritage home can significantly increase its market value.

It's also worth incorporating changes that allow for sustainable living. We believe modern living with a period façade is best achieved by prioritising flexibility. Careful planning and exquisite design can produce a contemporary yet classic abode.
