## Headline
Rendering and Solid Plastering Melbourne

External render, solid plaster and interior wall finishing. Done properly, then ready for paint.

## Page copy
A clean render finish depends on what's underneath it. Substrate preparation, the right mix, correct curing time, and attention at corners and junctions. Rush any of that and the cracks show up within a season.

We handle external acrylic rendering and solid plastering across Melbourne's inner south. New extensions, full facade refreshes, patch repairs, and interior wall finishing where a smooth, durable surface matters before paint or tiles go on.

In Melbourne's older homes this takes a different approach. Federation and Edwardian walls move. Lime-based repairs, breathable finishes, and matching existing profiles all matter. We've been working inside period housing stock for over 30 years and know how to treat it properly.

You brief us once. Nicon Built coordinates the right plasterers from a trusted network, sequences the work with painters, bricklayers and other trades, and makes sure every stage is finished to standard before the next one starts.

## FAQs

**What is the difference between rendering and solid plastering?** Rendering generally refers to the external cement or acrylic finish applied to brick, block or substrate walls. Solid plastering covers interior wall and ceiling finishing Ś the smooth plaster surface ready for paint or wallpaper. Nicon Built handles both, often as part of the same project.

**Can you repair cracked or damaged render?** Yes. Patch repairs, section replacements and full facade re-renders are all common jobs. We assess the substrate first so the repair holds rather than cracking again at the same point.

**Do you work on heritage and period homes?** Yes. Melbourne's Victorian, Edwardian and Federation homes often need lime-based repairs, breathable finishes and careful matching to existing profiles. Nicon Built has 30+ years of experience in period housing stock across the inner south.

**Can rendering be part of a larger renovation or extension?** Absolutely. Rendering is frequently sequenced with brickwork, concreting, painting and structural work. Nicon Built manages the full scope so each trade completes their stage in the right order.
