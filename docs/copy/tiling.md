## Page copy
Tiling is one of those jobs where the difference between good and average is obvious to anyone, even if they can't say exactly why. Inconsistent joints, lippage between tiles, grout that's a slightly wrong colour. You notice it every time you walk into the room.

We tile kitchens, bathrooms, laundries, outdoor entertaining areas, and feature walls across Melbourne's inner south. Floor and wall, large format and mosaic, period tiles in heritage homes where the job calls for it. Every job is prepared properly, waterproofed in wet areas, and laid to a standard that holds up over time.

For bathroom tiling especially, waterproofing is non-negotiable. If it's not done properly before the tiles go down, you'll have problems later that are expensive to fix. We don't cut that corner.

## FAQs

**Do you handle waterproofing before tiling?** Yes, always in wet areas. Waterproofing is applied to code before any tiles go down in bathrooms, ensuites, and laundries. It's not optional and we treat it as a non-negotiable step.

**Can you match existing tiles in a heritage or period home?** We do our best to source matching or complementary tiles for period homes. For heritage properties where the existing tiles are significant, we'll discuss the best approach before starting.

**Do you tile outdoor areas?** Yes. Outdoor entertaining areas and alfresco spaces. Substrate prep and grout selection for outdoor tiling is different from interior work and we treat it accordingly.

**Can tiling be coordinated as part of a renovation?** Yes. Tiling is a standard part of kitchen and bathroom renovations. Nick coordinates the sequencing so tiling happens at the right stage, after waterproofing and before fixtures are installed.
