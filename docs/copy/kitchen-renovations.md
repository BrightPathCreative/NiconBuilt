The kitchen is the room that gets used most and renovated most carelessly. Most of the problems come down to coordination. The tiler arrives before the plumber is done. The cabinet maker quotes for a space that doesn't match the structural reality. When a licensed builder is managing the job, none of that happens.

Nick coordinates every trade on every kitchen renovation he takes on. Plumbers, electricians, tilers, cabinet makers, plasterers, painters. All sequenced properly, all held to the same standard, all managed by the same person from start to finish.

In Melbourne's period homes, kitchen renovations require an extra level of care. Victorian, Edwardian, and Federation homes often have structural considerations that newer homes don't. Load-bearing walls, heritage overlay implications for certain alterations, original flooring that needs to be worked around rather than cut through. Nick has 30 years of experience managing exactly these situations.

You brief Nick on what you want. He assesses the space, gives you a straight quote, and manages the whole job from there. No dealing with individual tradespeople. One call, one point of accountability, one result you're happy with.

## FAQs

**Do I need a licensed builder for a kitchen renovation?** For straightforward kitchen updates, a builder's licence isn't always required. But most meaningful kitchen renovations involve structural work, plumbing relocation, or electrical changes that do require a licensed builder. Nick manages the full scope, so whatever the job involves, it's covered.

**How do you manage all the different trades?** Nick coordinates every trade himself. Plumbers, electricians, tilers, plasterers, cabinet makers, painters. All from a trusted network built up over 30 years. You brief Nick once and he handles the rest.

**How long does a kitchen renovation take?** A full kitchen renovation typically takes four to eight weeks from start to finish depending on scope and whether structural changes are required. Nick gives you a realistic timeline when he provides the quote.

**Do you do kitchen renovations in period homes?** Yes. Period homes are a specialist focus. Nick has 30 years of experience working inside Melbourne's inner-south heritage housing stock and understands what to look for before the job starts.
