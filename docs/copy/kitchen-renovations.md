The cabinet maker quotes for a space that doesn't match the structural reality. When a licensed builder is managing the job, none of that happens.

Nicon Built coordinates every trade on every kitchen renovation we take on. Plumbers, electricians, tilers, cabinet makers, plasterers, painters. All sequenced properly, all held to the same standard, all managed by the same team from start to finish.

In Melbourne's period homes, kitchen renovations require an extra level of care. Victorian, Edwardian, and Federation homes often have structural considerations that newer homes don't. Load-bearing walls, heritage overlay implications for certain alterations, original flooring that needs to be worked around rather than cut through. Nicon Built has 30 years of experience managing exactly these situations.

You brief us on what you want. We assess the space, give you a straight quote, and manage the whole job from there. No dealing with individual tradespeople. One call, one point of accountability, one result you're happy with.

## FAQs

**Do I need a licensed builder for a kitchen renovation?** For straightforward kitchen updates, a builder's licence isn't always required. But most meaningful kitchen renovations involve structural work, plumbing relocation, or electrical changes that do require a licensed builder. Nicon Built manages the full scope, so whatever the job involves, it's covered.

**How do you manage all the different trades?** Nicon Built coordinates every trade. Plumbers, electricians, tilers, plasterers, cabinet makers, painters. All from a trusted network built up over 30 years. You brief us once and we handle the rest.

**How long does a kitchen renovation take?** A full kitchen renovation typically takes four to eight weeks from start to finish depending on scope and whether structural changes are required. We give you a realistic timeline when we provide the quote.

**Do you do kitchen renovations in period homes?** Yes. Period homes are a specialist focus. Nicon Built has 30 years of experience working inside Melbourne's inner-south heritage housing stock and understands what to look for before the job starts.
